# CanonKit Market Analysis

## Bottom Line

CanonKit is worth publishing on GitHub because it targets a real workflow gap:

- mature open-source tools already cover drafting and outlining
- commercial tools prove that writers will pay for planning, canon, and AI context
- very few tools, especially open-source ones, make continuity QA the core product

That makes CanonKit a strong wedge: narrow enough to build now, broad enough to commercialize later.

## What Already Exists

### 1. Open-source drafting tools

Relevant references:

- [novelWriter](https://github.com/vkbo/novelWriter)
- [Manuskript](https://github.com/olivierkes/manuskript)
- [OpenWrite](https://github.com/ilrein/openwrite)

What they prove:

- local-first writing has a real audience
- long-form structure matters
- writers like tools they can trust and control

Where they are strongest:

- drafting
- chapter and scene organization
- notes and project structure

Where the gap remains:

- systematic continuity QA
- explicit canon conflict reporting
- scene-ready AI context packaging

### 2. Commercial planning and writing workspaces

Relevant references:

- [bibisco](https://www.bibisco.com/)
- [Story Architect](https://starc.app/)
- [Campfire Writing](https://www.campfirewriting.com/)
- [Plottr](https://plottr.com/)

What they prove:

- writers pay for project organization
- story planning and worldbuilding are valuable products on their own
- a writing workflow can be more than a text editor

Where they are strongest:

- planning
- metadata organization
- story structure
- worldbuilding

Where the gap remains:

- continuity QA is not always the primary surface
- open-source distribution is not the main growth channel
- many of the most valuable workflows are still closed

### 3. AI-first fiction products

Relevant references:

- [Sudowrite](https://www.sudowrite.com/)
- [NovelCrafter](https://www.novelcrafter.com/)

What they prove:

- authors will pay for reduced prompt friction
- better context management has direct value
- "memory" is a product feature, not just a backend concern

Where the gap remains:

- users still need a trusted canon source of truth
- continuity failures often come from weak project structure, not weak generation
- some authors want AI help without surrendering all project control to a hosted platform

## Why CanonKit Is A Better Open-source Bet Than "Another AI Novel Writer"

Building a generic "AI novel writing app" is a weaker move because:

- it competes in a crowded and expensive category
- the product story becomes vague very quickly
- most of the defensible value is hidden in hosted model orchestration

CanonKit is a better wedge because:

- the pitch is clear: story bible plus continuity checker plus context pack
- local-first is a trust advantage
- the MVP can be useful without any backend
- the repo can spread on GitHub without looking like a crippled SaaS teaser

## User Pain That CanonKit Directly Solves

Long-form fiction authors repeatedly run into:

- inconsistent ages and years
- relationship drift across revisions
- world rules that stop being enforced halfway through a draft
- scene notes that mention missing or renamed entities
- repetitive prompt assembly for AI-assisted drafting

These pains grow with:

- serial fiction
- multi-book projects
- collaborative editing
- AI-assisted revision loops

That makes continuity control a stable workflow problem, not a novelty feature.

## MVP Boundary

The right MVP is intentionally narrow:

- local project data
- characters, locations, rules, and scenes
- explicit continuity checks
- issue report UI
- scene-level AI context pack
- import and export later

What the MVP should not try to solve yet:

- full rich-text drafting
- team collaboration
- cloud accounts
- provider-heavy AI integrations
- complex plugin marketplaces

This keeps the product honest and shippable.

## Open Source And Commercialization Are Compatible Here

CanonKit has a clean split between open value and paid value.

Open-source core:

- trusted local data model
- continuity engine
- UI for canon inspection
- community rule templates

Commercial extensions:

- sync and backup
- multi-user collaboration
- editorial review workflows
- higher-cost AI analysis pipelines
- series-level canon graphs
- importer ecosystem

This is important because the GitHub version can be genuinely useful while still leaving room for a serious business.

## Risks

The main risks are:

- the checker becomes too shallow and feels cosmetic
- the checker becomes too heavy and slows down product focus
- the team drifts back toward "AI writes for you" and loses differentiation
- import and migration paths stay weak, making adoption harder

The answer is scope discipline:

- keep the wedge narrow
- make continuity checks real, not decorative
- treat AI as a consumer of canon, not the whole product

## Final Judgment

CanonKit is worth building and worth publishing on GitHub.

It sits in a credible gap between open-source drafting tools and closed AI writing platforms. The product story is understandable, the MVP is feasible, and the commercial path remains open after launch.
